High Pass Filters

A high pass filter removes low frequencies, letting only the highs "pass through."

Use it to clean up rumble from vocals or make room for bass instruments.

Low Pass Filters

A low pass filter removes high frequencies, letting only the lows "pass through."

This technique is common in electronic music to create lo-fi or underwater effects.

Scooping / Boosting

"Scooping" means cutting a narrow frequency range — often used to remove muddiness or harshness.

For example, cutting the 300-500Hz range can clear up a mix.

Boosting adds energy or presence to certain parts of a sound.

Boost with care — it's easy to go overboard.

Making Room

In a mix, different instruments compete for the same frequencies. EQ helps give each sound its own space.

  • Cut lows from piano so it doesn't fight the bass
  • Cut highs from bass to leave room for vocals
