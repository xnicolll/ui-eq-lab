EQ (Equalization) is the process of adjusting the loudness of different frequencies in an audio signal to achieve a more balanced sound.

When an audio signal is left untreated, there will often be clashing frequencies, or "not enough room" for every detail to be heard. It's the audio engineer's role to use EQing and decide on which details to highlight, and which to turn down.

With EQ, you can:

  • Turn up the bass
  • Soften harsh treble
  • Carve out space in a mix
  • You can do practically anything!

When mixing music, EQ helps instruments and vocals sit well together without clashing.

Mixing: the process of balancing, blending, and / or applying effects to individual audio tracks to create a cohesive sound.

Lows

Bass, Kick Drum
Mids
